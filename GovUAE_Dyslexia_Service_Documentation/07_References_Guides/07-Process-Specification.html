--- title: Dyslexia Monitoring & Intervention Process Specification version: 1.0 date_created: 2026-05-06 owner: Product & Clinical Advisory Team tags: [process, dyslexia, education, intervention, dislexia] --- # Specification: Dyslexia Monitoring & Intervention Process (DIMS) ## 1. Purpose & Scope This specification defines the end-to-end process for the Dyslexia Intelligence Monitoring System (DIMS), a 9-stage automated workflow for identifying, monitoring, and intervening in children (6-17 years) with dyslexia. The process encompasses: - **Initial identification** through educator referrals or parental concerns - **Cognitive assessment** via multidimensional battery of dyslexia-specific tests - **Automated diagnosis** using weighted scoring and AI-driven pattern analysis - **Personalized intervention planning** using Claude API (Hermes Agent) - **Continuous monitoring** with dynamic reclassification - **Impact evaluation** measuring longitudinal progress **Intended Audience:** Product engineers, clinical advisors, compliance officers, ONG coordinators, educators, specialist referral partners. **Assumptions:** - Children have access to devices with microphone/camera for audio/visual testing - Internet connectivity sufficient for real-time data transmission - Educators/parents can engage in brief referral/consent process - System operates in Portuguese, with multi-language extensibility planned --- ## 2. Definitions | Term | Definition | |------|-----------| | **Dyslexia** | Specific learning disability affecting accurate/fluent word recognition, decoding, and spelling despite adequate intelligence and instruction (DSM-5 aligned) | | **Phonological Processing** | Ability to manipulate and perceive sounds in language; primary deficit in phonological dyslexia | | **WPM** | Words Per Minute; fluency metric measured via 1-minute reading passages | | **RAN** | Rapid Automatized Naming; speed of accessing phonological labels (rapid naming of colors, objects) | | **Discrepancy Model** | Comparing listening comprehension vs. reading comprehension; gap >15 points suggests specific reading disorder | | **Pseudowords** | Non-words following phonotactic rules (e.g., "balt", "kif") to test phonetic decoding ability | | **Phonemic Awareness** | Conscious awareness of individual phonemes (sounds); prerequisite for alphabetic decoding | | **Acomodações** | Classroom modifications (extra time, font changes, alternative formats) to reduce impact of dyslexia without lowering academic standards | | **LGPD** | Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (Brazil data protection law); requires explicit consent for processing children's personal data | | **Hermes Agent** | Claude API-based reasoning agent for generating personalized intervention recommendations | | **Reclassification** | Dynamic reassignment to new risk tier based on updated assessment scores | --- ## 3. Requirements, Constraints & Guidelines ### FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS **REQ-001: Multi-Stage Process Flow** - System SHALL execute 9 sequential and/or parallel stages - Each stage SHALL have explicit entry/exit criteria - Each stage SHALL generate timestamped logs for audit trail **REQ-002: Multidimensional Assessment** - System SHALL administer minimum 4 cognitive dimensions: - Phonological processing (40% weight) - Reading fluency/WPM (35% weight) - Reading comprehension (15% weight) - Visual-orthographic processing (10% weight) - System SHALL measure and flag discrepancy between listening vs. reading comprehension - Composite score SHALL range 0-100 with >3 decimal places for sensitivity **REQ-003: Automated Risk Classification** - System SHALL classify children into 5 tiers: No Risk (0-30), Low Risk (31-50), Moderate Risk (51-70), High Risk (71-85), Critical/Confirmed (>85) - Classification logic SHALL be transparent and auditable - System SHALL flag required actions for each tier (monitoring vs. intervention vs. specialist referral) **REQ-004: Dynamic Reclassification** - System SHALL reclassify child if score changes >10 points - System SHALL reclassify if 3+ weeks pass without progress data - System SHALL trigger alerts if reclassification changes intervention intensity **REQ-005: AI-Driven Intervention Planning** - System SHALL generate personalized intervention plans within 5 minutes using Hermes Agent - Plans SHALL include: - Phase-based structure (consciousness, decoding, fluency, comprehension) - Specific activity recommendations tied to observed deficits - Frequency and duration targets - Clear responsibility assignment (educator, specialist, parent) - Weekly progress milestones - Plans SHALL be reviewed by human psychopedagogue before delivery **REQ-006: Continuous Monitoring Loop** - System SHALL collect progress data weekly or bi-weekly (configurable) - Progress checks SHALL include: WPM re-measurement, targeted sub-skill assessment, adherence tracking - System SHALL detect regression (WPM decline, score increase) and trigger escalation **REQ-007: Multi-Channel Notifications** - System SHALL deliver: - SMS/WhatsApp to caregivers (progress, motivation, reminders) - Email to educators (strategies, recommendations, performance reports) - Email to specialists (if referral required, with full assessment data) - Dashboard notifications to ONG coordinator (caseload health, escalations) - Notifications SHALL be templated and personalizable **REQ-008: Longitudinal Reporting** - System SHALL generate comparison reports: - Baseline vs. current scores (absolute and relative change) - WPM progression trend (linear regression) - Comorbidity indicators (TDAH, TEA screening flags) - Adherence metrics (% of scheduled sessions attended) - ROI estimation (cost of intervention vs. social impact) ### NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS **NFR-001: Performance** - Stage 3 (cognitive battery) SHALL complete in 40-45 minutes total - Stage 5 (scoring/analysis) SHALL complete in <2 seconds - Stage 6 (plan generation) SHALL complete in <5 minutes - All user-facing actions SHALL have <2 second response time (95th percentile) **NFR-002: Availability** - System SHALL maintain 99.5% uptime SLA - Real-time data collection SHALL have automatic retry logic (exponential backoff, max 3 attempts) - If stage fails, system SHALL escalate to human reviewer within 1 hour **NFR-003: Security** - All PII (child name, test data, audio) SHALL be encrypted AES-256 at rest - All data transmission SHALL use TLS 1.3 - Access SHALL be role-based (educator, specialist, ONG admin) - Audio recordings SHALL be deleted after scoring (retain only anonymized features) - System SHALL NOT expose individual child identifiers to third-party services **NFR-004: Compliance** - System SHALL obtain explicit LGPD consent before any assessment - System SHALL allow data subject (parent/guardian) to request deletion of their child's data (right to be forgotten) - System SHALL maintain audit log of all data access (who, when, what) - System SHALL NOT share data with external specialists without explicit parental consent - System SHALL include safeguarding protocols: if abuse indicators detected in free-text responses, SHALL escalate to trained social worker **NFR-005: Usability** - Interface SHALL be accessible (WCAG 2.1 AA) for children 6-17 with diverse literacy levels - Reading level of instructions SHALL not exceed 4th grade (Flesch-Kincaid) - System SHALL support both keyboard and voice input - System SHALL provide real-time feedback (progress bars, encouragement) to maintain engagement ### DESIGN CONSTRAINTS **CON-001: Single Point of Truth for Assessment** - Child may NOT complete the same test stage twice within 3 days (avoid practice effects) - Exception: If technical failure occurs, automatic retry allowed within 24 hours - If child fails 3+ times, escalate to specialist **CON-002: Specialist Review Gate** - Any child classified >70 (High Risk/Critical) SHALL be reviewed by psychopedagogue before notifying family - Specialist review SHALL include: score verification, pattern analysis, differential diagnosis considerations **CON-003: Acomodations Consistency** - Once acomodations are assigned (e.g., extra time, dyslexia-friendly font), SHALL be applied consistently across all assessments - Any change to acomodations SHALL be documented with rationale **CON-004: Intervention Intensity Thresholds** - Moderate Risk (51-70): 2-3 sessions/week, 3-6 month duration - High Risk (71-85): 4-5 sessions/week, 6-12 month duration - Critical (>85): Daily + specialist involvement, 12+ months - Deviations from these require specialist approval --- ## 4. Acceptance Criteria **AC-001: Child Registration** - Given a valid referral from educator, When parent/guardian submits registration, Then system SHALL create child profile with all mandatory fields, validate LGPD consent, and trigger Stage 2 within 24 hours **AC-002: Assessment Battery Completion** - Given Stage 3 initiated, When child completes all 3 sessions (phonology, fluency, comprehension, visual), Then system SHALL store all raw scores, calculate composite metrics, and validate data quality (no missing values) before advancing to Stage 5 **AC-003: Risk Scoring Accuracy** - Given completed assessment data, When system calculates overall risk score, Then: - Score SHALL match manual calculation (educator validation) within ±3 points - Classification tier SHALL be correctly assigned per thresholds (0-30 = No Risk, etc.) - Discrepancy flag SHALL trigger specialist review if >70 **AC-004: Plan Generation & Review** - Given High Risk child (score >65), When Hermes Agent generates intervention plan, Then: - Plan SHALL be reviewed by psychopedagogue within 2 hours - Plan SHALL include minimum 3 intervention phases with specific activities - Plan SHALL assign clear responsibility (Educator/Specialist/Parent) - Plan SHALL not be delivered to family until reviewed **AC-005: Notification Delivery** - Given plan approval, When notifications triggered, Then: - SMS/WhatsApp to caregiver delivered within 1 hour - Email to educator delivered within 1 hour - Delivery confirmation logged in system **AC-006: Continuous Monitoring** - Given active intervention (Stages 8-9), When weekly progress collection scheduled, Then: - System SHALL prompt caregiver/educator for WPM re-test - System SHALL update score and flag if reclassification required - System SHALL alert if >3 weeks without data (intervention at risk) **AC-007: Longitudinal Impact Report** - Given child at 6-month exit point, When final assessment triggered, Then: - Report SHALL show baseline vs. exit scores for all dimensions - Report SHALL calculate WPM delta (absolute and %) - Report SHALL estimate ROI based on intervention cost and estimated social/economic impact - Report SHALL recommend continuation, step-down, or specialist follow-up **AC-008: Data Privacy & Security** - Given any child assessment in system, When data access requested, Then: - System SHALL verify requestor role (educator/specialist/parent) - Audio recordings SHALL be deleted after scoring (retain features only) - All access SHALL be logged with timestamp, user ID, data accessed - System SHALL support parent's LGPD right to deletion (anonymize data) --- ## 6. Rationale & Context ### Why 9 Stages? The original 7-stage model was insufficient for dyslexia's complexity: - **Stages 1-3**: Identification & assessment (same as general child monitoring) - **Stages 4-7**: Diagnosis, planning, notification, monitoring (specific to dyslexia) - **Stages 8-9** (new): Continuous progress tracking & longitudinal impact (critical because dyslexia is a developmental trajectory, not a point diagnosis) Dyslexia requires **longitudinal monitoring** beyond initial diagnosis because: 1. Intervention effectiveness varies by individual 2. Comorbidities (ADHD, ASD) may emerge over time 3. School demands (reading load) increase with age, potentially unmasking deficits ### Why Multidimensional Assessment? Single-metric approaches (e.g., WPM alone) create false negatives: - **Phonological dyslexia** (most common): High WPM but poor accuracy (many errors) → WPM alone misses it - **Surface dyslexia**: Can decode phonetically but can't recognize irregular words → different intervention needed - **Double deficit**: Both phonological AND fluency deficits → require combined intervention **Weights chosen:** - Phonological (40%): Primary deficit in ~70% of dyslexia cases - Fluency (35%): Functional impact (reading speed affects school participation) - Comprehension (15%): Marker of compensation (some kids decode but don't understand) - Visual-orthographic (10%): Sub-type identifier ### Why Audiovisual Discrepancy as Key Marker? Children with dyslexia typically have: - **Strong listening comprehension** (can understand language when spoken) - **Weak reading comprehension** (can't access meaning from written text) A gap >15 points = **specific reading disorder** (vs. general language impairment). This discrepancy distinguishes dyslexia from: - Intellectual disability (deficits across domains) - Language disorder (listening & reading both weak) - ADHD (attention affects both, but no specific phonological deficit) ### Why AI-Driven Personalized Planning? Standard intervention manuals (one-size-fits-all) are **ineffective for dyslexia heterogeneity**: - Child A: Pure phonological deficit → needs explicit phonological intervention - Child B: Comorbid with ADHD → needs shorter, more frequent sessions with movement breaks - Child C: Has compensatory strength in visual memory → can use multisensory approach Hermes Agent analyzes the full **assessment profile** and generates targeted recommendations: - Phase structure adapted to deficit severity - Activity selection based on observed strengths/weaknesses - Frequency/duration calibrated to comorbidities - Materials recommended based on engagement markers ### Why Dynamic Reclassification? Dyslexia intervention response varies widely: - **Fast responders** (40%): Show measurable progress within 4 weeks → can reduce intervention intensity - **Slow responders** (40%): Show progress by 8-12 weeks → maintain or escalate intervention - **Non-responders** (20%): Minimal progress → may require specialist evaluation or modified approach System must continuously reassess and **adapt intensity** to actual response, not static initial classification. --- ## 8. Examples & Edge Cases ### Example 1: High-Risk Dyslexia Child with ADHD Comorbidity ``` INPUT (Assessment Results): ├─ Phonological Score: 78 (high deficit) ├─ WPM: 35 (severely below norm for 9-year-old, who should be ~120) ├─ Listening Comprehension: 92 ├─ Reading Comprehension: 45 ├─ Audiovisual Discrepancy: 47 points (SEVERE) ├─ Behavioral Notes: "Fidgety, trouble staying focused on reading task" SYSTEM PROCESSING: ├─ Overall Risk Score: 82 (HIGH RISK / CRITICAL) ├─ Primary Deficit: Phonological dyslexia ├─ Comorbidity Flag: ADHD risk (behavioral observation + discrepancy pattern) AI-GENERATED PLAN: ├─ Phase 1: Phonemic Awareness (4 weeks) │ └─ Frequency: 3x/week, 10 minute sessions (shorter due to ADHD) │ └─ Strategy: Include movement breaks (jumping for syllables, clapping for phonemes) │ └─ Engagement: Gamified (earning points for focus) │ ├─ Phase 2: Phonetic Decoding (weeks 5-8) │ └─ Frequency: 4x/week, 15 minute sessions │ └─ Strategy: Multi-sensory (letter shapes, letter sounds, writing) │ └─ Engagement: Story-based progression (build story as sounds are learned) │ ├─ Phase 3: Fluency (weeks 9-16) │ └─ Frequency: 5x/week, 20 minute sessions (gradually increasing) │ └─ Strategy: Partner reading (child reads with model, builds confidence) │ └─ Engagement: Choose own books at reading level │ ├─ Acomodations: │ ├─ Extra time on school tests (50%) │ ├─ Dyslexia-friendly font + increased spacing │ ├─ Oral responses allowed │ └─ Movement breaks during long reading sessions │ ├─ CRITICAL NOTE: │ "Recommend parallel ADHD evaluation by pediatric neuropsychologist. │ If ADHD confirmed, address concurrently (short sessions, high engagement, │ movement integration). Dyslexia intervention alone may not resolve difficulty │ if attention is the limiting factor." │ └─ Monitoring: └─ Bi-weekly check-ins (given comorbidity risk, higher supervision) └─ ADHD symptoms to track: On-task time, impulse control during tasks └─ Dyslexia progress: WPM weekly, phonological bi-weekly └─ If ADHD medication initiated, adjust phase intensity (may see sudden improvement) OUTPUT (To Stakeholders): ├─ Parents: "João shows signs of both reading disability and attention challenges. We recommend evaluation by neuropsychologist while starting reading support now. Plan includes short sessions with movement to help focus." ├─ Educator: [Detailed plan + acomodations checklist] └─ ONG Coordinator: "Escalate to specialist for ADHD assessment + continue reading intervention" ``` ### Example 2: Surface Dyslexia (Irregular Word Weakness) ``` INPUT: ├─ Phonological Score: 42 (NORMAL) ├─ WPM: 85 (NORMAL for 8-year-old, ~90) ├─ Pseudo-word Reading: 92 (STRONG phonetic decoding) ├─ Irregular Word Reading: 30 (SEVERE weakness) ├─ Strategy Observation: Child sounded out "yacht" as "yak-t", couldn't recognize "said" DIAGNOSIS: ├─ Risk Score: 35 (LOW RISK, but not zero) ├─ Pattern: Surface dyslexia (strong phonetic decoding, weak visual/memory access) ├─ Implication: Child CAN decode but has weak sight word bank AI PLAN: ├─ Focus: NOT phonological intervention (not needed) ├─ Focus: Visual memory, whole-word recognition, etymology ├─ Activities: │ ├─ Irregular word flashcards with story/image associations │ ├─ Etymology study (high-frequency irregular words share patterns: said/paid/laid) │ └─ Contextual reading (meaning aids recognition of irregular words) ├─ Frequency: 2x/week, 15 minutes (less intensive than phonological) └─ Expected Progress: +30 WPM, but improvement focused on accuracy, not speed ``` ### Example 3: Non-Responsive Child (No Progress After 4 Weeks) ``` TRIGGER: ├─ Child in Moderate Risk intervention for 4 weeks ├─ Sessions attended: 8/12 (67% adherence) ├─ WPM baseline: 45 → current: 48 (minimal change) ├─ Phonological Score: unchanged ├─ ALERT: "No meaningful progress. Escalate." SYSTEM RESPONSE: ├─ Automatic escalation checklist: │ ├─ Review adherence: 67% acceptable, but <75% target │ ├─ Check implementation fidelity: Were activities done as planned? │ ├─ Assess external factors: School transition? New sibling? Family stress? │ ├─ Verify assessment quality: Was baseline assessment valid? │ └─ Consider comorbidity: Has ADHD/anxiety/depression emerged? │ ├─ AI Generates Updated Recommendation: │ ├─ "Adherence at 67% may be limiting. Recommend: │ │ 1. Assess barriers (too many sessions, distance, motivation) │ │ 2. Simplify plan: Reduce to 2x/week to ensure quality │ │ 3. Increase engagement: Use gamification, child's interest (sports, music) │ │ 4. Screen for ADHD (ADHD + dyslexia may require different approach) │ └─ 5. Phonological score elevated due to │ not yet knowing all letters, not due to dyslexia deficit." │ ├─ Decision: "Not dyslexia. Developmentally normal progress for early reader. │ Recommend regular classroom instruction. Validation Criteria ✓ **Assessment Validity** - External validation: 90%+ agreement between system scoring and manual expert scoring on 50 test cases - Criterion validity: Score >70 on system correlates with positive specialist diagnosis (r > 0.85) - Test-retest reliability: Same child tested 6 months later shows stable classification (>80% remain in same tier) ✓ **Intervention Effectiveness** - Outcome metric: >60% of children improve by ≥10 points within 6 months - Average improvement: 20+ WPM by 6 months - Adherence: ≥75% session attendance correlates with meaningful progress ✓ **Plan Relevance** - Educator feedback: ≥80% report plan activities are "very relevant" to observed deficits - Implementation fidelity: ≥80% of planned activities successfully completed ✓ **Usability** - Assessment completion: ≥90% of children complete full battery without dropout - System availability: 99.5% uptime measured monthly ✓ **Safety & Compliance** - LGPD: 100% of children have documented consent before assessment - Data security: 0 unauthorized access incidents, 0 data breaches - Escalation: 100% of abuse indicators escalated to social worker within 1 hour --- ## 11.
