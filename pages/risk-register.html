RISK-001: False Positive Diagnosis (Misclassification)

Owner: Assessment Specialist Lead
Domain: Clinical Quality

Description: System classifies child as having dyslexia (score >70) when child is actually early reader or has different diagnosis (language disorder, ADHD). False positives cause psychological harm (stigma) + unnecessary intervention. Current State:

  • No specialist validation gate (anyone can receive diagnosis)
  • No differential diagnosis process
  • No accuracy validation (compared to clinical reference)
  • No feedback loop (don't know false positive rate) Impact:
  • Probability: HIGH (20-30% of high-risk cases are false positives)
  • Consequence: Harm to child (stigma, unnecessary label) + Waste of intervention resources ($100-200 per false positive) + Program credibility damage + Legal risk (LGPD complaint if mislabeling caused harm)
  • Overall Impact Score: 5/5 (SEVERE) Risk Score: 4 x 5 = 20 (CRITICAL) Mitigation Strategy:
  • IMMEDIATE (Week 1-2):
  • Implement specialist review gate: * All scores >70 require psychopedagogue review (48h max) * Specialist checks: phonological deficit is real? * Rules out: intellectual disability, language disorder, ADHD
  • SHORT-TERM (Month 1-2):
  • Validate assessment accuracy: * Compare system scores to 50 clinical specialist diagnoses * Calculate sensitivity/specificity * Adjust algorithm if <90% accuracy
  • ONGOING:
  • Track false positive rate: * Every child classified >70 gets "validation check" at 6 months * If improvement not as expected → likely false positive * Retrospective analysis of misclassifications
  • Success Metric: False positive rate <5% (current: ~20%) Monitoring:
  • Weekly: Cases pending specialist review (should be <48h)
  • Monthly: False positive rate (should be declining)
  • Quarterly: Validation accuracy vs. reference standard

Status: IN PROGRESS (Specialist gate partially implemented)
Next Review: 2026-05-20

RISK-002: LGPD/Data Privacy Breach

Owner: Data Governance Officer
Domain: Compliance & Security

Description: Child PII (name, assessment results, audio recordings) exposed due to:

  • Unauthorized access
  • Unencrypted data transmission
  • Insecure storage
  • Accidental disclosure
  • Third-party partner breach
  • Malware/ransomware Impact:
  • Probability: MEDIUM (2-5% annual risk for platforms this size)
  • Consequence: * LGPD fines: up to R$ 50M or 2% of revenue * Reputational damage (parents lose trust) * Program shutdown (could lose service funding) * Child psychological harm (privacy violation)
  • Overall Impact Score: 5/5 (CATASTROPHIC) Risk Score: 3 x 5 = 15 (CRITICAL) Mitigation Strategy:
  • IMMEDIATE (Week 1-4):
  • Encrypt all PII: AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit
  • Implement access controls: Azure AD RBAC
  • Audit all data access: Logging to Azure Monitor
  • Delete audio recordings after scoring (retain features only)
  • Digital consent management: Can't process without signed LGPD consent
  • SHORT-TERM (Month 1-3):
  • Security audit by external firm (penetration testing)
  • LGPD compliance certification (external audit)
  • Data governance policy documentation
  • Staff training on LGPD (100% completion)
  • ONGOING:
  • Monthly security updates (patches, OS upgrades)
  • Quarterly penetration testing
  • Semi-annual disaster recovery test
  • Annual external security audit
  • Incident response plan (tested quarterly)
  • Success Metric: Zero data breaches, 100% LGPD compliance certification Monitoring:
  • Weekly: Access logs review (any anomalies?)
  • Monthly: Compliance checklist
  • Quarterly: Security audit results
  • Annual: External certification audit

Status: IN PROGRESS (Encryption implemented, audit scheduled)
Next Review: 2026-05-13

RISK-003: Low Adoption / Program Abandonment

Owner: Program Manager
Domain: Operational Effectiveness

Description: ONGs, educators, or parents don't adopt the system because:

  • Too complex (high cognitive load)
  • Doesn't integrate with existing workflows
  • Perceived lack of value (no visible impact)
  • Cultural resistance (prefer manual processes)
  • Inadequate training/support
  • Technology barriers (poor connectivity, device access)
  • Competing priorities (other urgent programs) Impact:
  • Probability: MEDIUM-HIGH (40-60% of transformations have adoption issues)
  • Consequence: * Program doesn't reach target scale (serves <50% potential children) * High implementation cost but low ROI * Loss of educator buy-in → low referral quality * Funding risk (donors expect usage metrics) * Opportunity cost (could have built different solution)
  • Overall Impact Score: 4/5 (SEVERE) Risk Score: 3 x 4 = 12 (CRITICAL) Mitigation Strategy:
  • IMMEDIATE (Week 1-2):
  • Stakeholder engagement plan: Involve educators/ONGs in design
  • Use case development: Document "day in the life" workflows
  • Low-fidelity prototypes: Test with 2-3 early adopter ONGs
  • Feedback loops: Integrate feedback into system design
  • SHORT-TERM (Month 1-3):
  • Change management program: * Communication plan (weekly updates) * Training plan (per-role certification) * Support plan (24/7 help desk)
  • Gamification: Leaderboards, badges, recognition for early adopters
  • Value demonstration: * Dashboard showing program impact (child progress) * ROI calculator (cost saved vs. manual processes) * Case studies (stories of children helped)
  • Reduce friction: * Mobile app (easier than web portal) * Offline capability (sync when connected) * SMS/WhatsApp integration (use tools they already use)
  • ONGOING:
  • Monthly adoption metrics: * Active users (should grow 20-30% per month initially) * Session frequency (educators should log in 2-3x/week) * Feature adoption (% using key features)
  • Quarterly feedback surveys: * What's working? What's not? * Barriers to adoption? * Feature requests?
  • Annual adoption review: * Total registered users vs. target * Active usage rate (% of registered actually using) * Cohort retention (% still active after 6 months)
  • Success Metric: >80% adoption among target users by Month 6 Monitoring:
  • Weekly: New user registrations (should see steady growth)
  • Bi-weekly: Support ticket trends (decreasing = better UX)
  • Monthly: Adoption metrics dashboard
  • Quarterly: Feedback survey & satisfaction (NPS >60)

Status: PLANNING (Change management team forming)
Next Review: 2026-05-13

RISK-004: Assessment Tool Accuracy / Algorithmic Bias

Owner: Clinical Advisory Lead
Domain: Clinical Quality

Description: System shows bias in scoring/classification by:

  • Gender (over-identifies girls, under-identifies boys, or vice versa)
  • Race/Ethnicity (cultural biases in test items)
  • Language (different languages have different phonological structures)
  • Socioeconomic status (SES correlates with test performance independent of dyslexia)
  • Neurodiversity (ADHD, ASD, anxiety confound scoring)
  • Age (developmental differences within age band not captured) Impact:
  • Probability: MEDIUM (20-30% of assessment tools have bias)
  • Consequence: * Some children systematically over/under-classified * Inequitable access to services (bias-affected groups get worse outcomes) * Legal/equity complaints (program discriminates) * Clinical harm (biased diagnosis could harm child psychology) * Program credibility damage
  • Overall Impact Score: 4/5 (SEVERE) Risk Score: 3 x 4 = 12 (CRITICAL) Mitigation Strategy:
  • IMMEDIATE (Week 1-4):
  • Bias audit of assessment items: * Review all test items with diverse panel (educators, linguists, diversity officer) * Identify items with cultural/language bias * Revise or remove biased items
  • Collect baseline demographic data: * Score distribution by gender, race, language * Identify disparities
  • Establish fairness thresholds: * Acceptable difference in classification rate by group: <5% * Sensitivity by group should be similar (+/-5%)
  • SHORT-TERM (Month 1-2):
  • External bias audit by psychometrician: * Compare to published dyslexia assessment tools (e.g., CTOPP, TOWRE) * Validate across languages (Portuguese, indigenous languages, etc.)
  • Subgroup analysis: * Calculate accuracy separately for each demographic group * Identify any group with lower accuracy
  • Algorithm adjustment: * If bias found: adjust weights, add group-specific norms, or revise items
  • Prospective fairness monitoring: * Monthly dashboard showing scores by demographic group * Alert if group shows disparate impact
  • ONGOING:
  • Quarterly bias audit: * Distribution of classifications by group * Accuracy measures by group * Any new disparities?
  • Annual external validation: * Third-party psychometrician review
  • Continuous improvement: * ML fairness constraints in scoring algorithm * Regular re-norming as population evolves
  • Success Metric: No significant bias detected; All groups classified with >90% accuracy Monitoring:
  • Baseline: Current accuracy by group (gender, race, language)
  • Monthly: Disparate impact analysis
  • Quarterly: Subgroup accuracy review
  • Annual: External psychometric audit

Status: PLANNING (Bias audit team forming)
Next Review: 2026-05-20

RISK-005: Integration Failure (Oracle/ServiceNow/Azure)

Owner: Technology Lead
Domain: Technology & Operations

Description: System integrations fail to work because:

  • APIs not available / changed by vendors
  • Data mapping incorrect (mismatched field definitions)
  • Performance issues (slow response times)
  • Synchronization failures (data not syncing between systems)
  • Service outages (vendor SLA breaches)
  • Incompatible versions (libraries, APIs outdated) Impact:
  • Probability: MEDIUM-HIGH (40% of integrations have initial issues)
  • Consequence: * Service degradation (system partially unavailable) * Manual workarounds required (defeats automation benefit) * Data inconsistency (child record conflicts between systems) * Operational delays (SLAs missed due to slow system) * Staff frustration / support burden increases
  • Overall Impact Score: 4/5 (SEVERE) Risk Score: 3 x 4 = 12 (CRITICAL) Mitigation Strategy:
  • IMMEDIATE (Week 1-2):
  • API integration planning: * Map all required API integrations * Document API contracts (request/response formats) * Identify error handling logic * Plan for API version changes
  • Data mapping validation: * Map Oracle → ServiceNow → Azure (field by field) * Identify data transformations needed * Document assumptions
  • Testing plan: * Unit tests for each API call * Integration tests for data flow * Load tests (expected volume) * Failover tests (what if API fails?)
  • SHORT-TERM (Month 1-3):
  • Prototype integrations: * Build POC for each major integration * Test with real API endpoints * Measure performance
  • Error handling & resilience: * Implement retry logic (exponential backoff) * Message queues for async processing * Fallback mechanisms (what if API is down?)
  • Monitoring & alerting: * Log all API calls and failures * Alert if API response time exceeds threshold * Dashboard showing integration health
  • Vendor management: * Establish SLA with each vendor * Regular check-ins on API stability * Participate in vendor roadmap planning
  • ONGOING:
  • Monthly integration health review: * API uptime metrics (99.5% target) * Data sync accuracy (100% target) * Performance metrics (response times)
  • Quarterly vendor reviews: * Any API changes coming? * Any SLA issues? * Feedback on reliability
  • Annual integration validation: * Re-test all integrations * Update documentation
  • Success Metric: 99.5% integration uptime, <100ms API response time Monitoring:
  • Real-time: API monitoring dashboard
  • Daily: Failed API calls log
  • Weekly: Integration health review
  • Monthly: SLA compliance metrics
  • Quarterly: Vendor performance review

Status: PLANNING (Integration design in progress)
Next Review: 2026-05-20

RISK-006: Specialist Availability / Capacity Shortage

Owner: ONG Coordinator
Domain: Operational Effectiveness

Description: Not enough qualified specialists (psychopedagogues, speech therapists) available to:

  • Review high-risk cases (>70) within 48h SLA
  • Conduct specialist assessments (in-person for cases that fail digital assessment)
  • Provide mentoring/guidance to educators
  • Maintain quality standards
  • Scale program to new regions Current State:
  • Only 15 available specialists across 5 states
  • Each specialist can review max 5-10 cases per day
  • Demand: 10,000 children/year x 30% high-risk = 3,000 reviews/year
  • Supply: 15 specialists x 250 work days x 7 cases/day = 26,250 cases/year
  • Apparent oversupply, BUT: * Specialists geographically concentrated (Sao Paulo, Brasilia) * Many are part-time or aging out * High turnover (burnout) * Competing demands (private practice, hospitals) Impact:
  • Probability: MEDIUM-HIGH (real capacity shortage in many regions)
  • Consequence: * Cases delayed >48h waiting for specialist review * Quality of reviews suffers (rushed assessment) * Some cases approved without review (quality gate failure) * Program can't scale beyond current capacity * Educator complaints (lack of specialist support)
  • Overall Impact Score: 4/5 (SEVERE) Risk Score: 3 x 4 = 12 (CRITICAL) Mitigation Strategy:
  • IMMEDIATE (Week 1-4):
  • Capacity audit: * Survey all specialists: availability, capacity, burnout level * Map specialists by geography * Identify critical gaps
  • Demand management: * Reduce specialist load by improving system accuracy (fewer >70 scores = fewer reviews needed) * Triage non-critical cases (some could be reviewed by trained psychopedagogues)
  • Retention program: * Identify at-risk specialists (considering retirement, burnout) * Offer incentives (flexible hours, CME credits, recognition) * Mentorship program (pair experienced with junior specialists)
  • SHORT-TERM (Month 1-6):
  • Workforce development: * Partner with universities to fast-track specialist training * Offer scholarships for students to specialize in dyslexia * Create "specialist-in-training" program (mentored by experienced specialists)
  • Role expansion: * Train advanced psychopedagogues to review lower-risk cases (<75) * Create tiered review process (not all need MD-level expertise)
  • Remote specialist network: * Build national/regional specialist roster * Can provide telehealth reviews (reduce travel burden) * Cross-region sharing (busy regions refer to less busy ones)
  • ONGOING:
  • Quarterly capacity planning: * Forecast demand (based on referral trends) * Compare to available capacity * Adjust hiring/training targets
  • Specialist burnout monitoring: * Quarterly surveys (job satisfaction, burnout index) * Identify at-risk individuals * Proactive retention actions
  • Continuing education: * Monthly specialist webinars (new findings, best practices) * Annual conference attendance subsidized * CME credits offered
  • Success Metric: 100% of >70 cases reviewed within 48h, zero backlog Monitoring:
  • Weekly: Cases pending specialist review (should be <48h)
  • Monthly: Specialist utilization rate (% capacity used)
  • Quarterly: Specialist satisfaction survey
  • Annual: Demand vs. supply forecast

Status: PLANNING (Capacity audit scheduled)
Next Review: 2026-05-20
