BASIC SERVICE INFORMATION

Service Name

Dyslexia Intelligence Monitoring System (DIMS) — Intelligent Dyslexia Monitoring and Intervention System

Main Objective

Identify, monitor, and intervene in children (6-17 years) with developmental dyslexia through an automated continuous diagnostic system, specialized cognitive tests, and generation of personalized intervention plans based on artificial intelligence, ensuring early detection, longitudinal monitoring, and compliance with LGPD.

Executive Description

The DIMS is a 9-stage platform that automates the complete dyslexia monitoring cycle:

  1. Initial Identification → Alert symptom collection (educator feedback)
  2. Behavioral Screening → Dyslexia questionnaire (DSM-5 aligned)
  3. Assisted Cognitive Assessment → Automated test batteries (WPM, fluency, comprehension)
  4. Phonological Processing Analysis → Specific deficit detection
  5. Preliminary Diagnosis Generation → Multidimensional scores with AI
  6. Individualized Plan Development → Pedagogical recommendations by Hermes Agent
  7. Multi-Channel Notification → Communication with all stakeholders
  8. Continuous Monitoring → Periodic tests with progress analysis
  9. Exit Assessment → Intervention impact and future recommendations

INPUTS (What Initiates the Process)

Triggers / Initiating Events

  1. Educator referral → Teacher observes alert signs (recurring reading difficulty, letter confusion, slowness)
  2. Parental suspicion → Parents concerned with reading development
  3. Preventive school assessment → Bulk screening at partner school
  4. Clinical referral → Prior diagnosis requiring continuous monitoring
  5. Periodic reassessment → Child in program requests reassessment (quarterly/semi-annual)
  6. Regression detection → System flags significant score deterioration

Mandatory Input Data (STAGE 1 & 2)

DEMOGRAPHIC and HISTORICAL data
  • Full name and date of birth (6-17 years)
  • Gender and laterality (right-handed/left-handed/ambidextrous)
  • Family history of dyslexia (first/second generation)
  • Native language(s) and secondary language(s)
  • History of otitis media (relevant for phonological processing)
  • Current grade and year
  • History of grade retention
  • Previous evaluations (psychopedagogical, speech-language, psychological) OBSERVED ALERT SIGNS (STAGE 2 - Questionnaire)
  • Difficulty recognizing rhymes
  • Slowness in reading (WPM abnormally low for age)
  • Letter or word inversion (b/d, sequences)
  • Difficulty spelling
  • Reading comprehension vs. auditory comprehension discrepancy
  • History of speech delay (early indicator)
  • Difficulty with syllable division
  • Reluctance to read or avoidance of reading tasks GUARDIAN DATA AND CONSENT
  • Name and contact of the legal guardian
  • Email and WhatsApp for notifications
  • Explicit LGPD consent for collection and analysis
  • Permission for sharing with educators/specialists
  • Authorization for reading/audio data storage EDUCATIONAL CONTEXT
  • Name of the school/ONG
  • Educator responsible for the referral
  • Reading teaching method used (phonic, global, etc.)
  • Pedagogical resources already used
  • Previous school test results (if available)

Specific Tests (STAGE 3 - Assisted Cognitive Assessment)

MULTIDIMENSIONAL TEST BATTERY FOR DYSLEXIA 1. PHONOLOGICAL PROCESSING (Weight 40%)
  • Phonemic Awareness Task
  • Identify, isolate, manipulate initial/final phonemes
  • Implicit Rhyme Test
  • Recognize rhyme patterns (estimated vs. real)
  • Syllable Segmentation
  • Divide words into syllables (production + identification)
  • Syllable Reversal
  • Reorganize syllables in reverse sequence 2. READING SPEED AND FLUENCY (Weight 35%)
  • WPM Test (Words Per Minute)
  • 1-minute test-retest passage
  • Comparison with age norms
  • Error count (omissions, insertions, substitutions)
  • Non-word Reading (Pseudowords)
  • Ability to apply phonological rules to novel stimuli
  • Irregular Word Reading
  • Visual recognition capacity (whole-word)
  • Rapid Automatized Naming (RAN)
  • Speed of lexical access 3. READING COMPREHENSION (Weight 15%)
  • Multiple choice questions about read passage
  • Inference and connection of ideas
  • Detail retention (literal vs. inferential)
  • Comparison: reading comprehension vs. auditory comprehension 4. VISUAL-ORTHOGRAPHIC PROCESSING (Weight 10%)
  • Visual Choice Test (choose correct word between similar options)
  • Visual sequence memory
  • Letter symmetry and orientation
  • Orthographic pattern recognition TIMING AND SEQUENCE OF TESTS
  • Session 1 (10-15 min): Phonemic Awareness + RAN
  • Session 2 (10-15 min): WPM + Pseudo-words
  • Session 3 (10-15 min): Comprehension + Visual Processing
  • Total: ~40-45 minutes in 3 sessions (reduces fatigue)

DIAGNOSTIC DIMENSIONS (Multidimensional Profile)

Phonological Score (0-100)
  • 0-30: Normal - Adequate phonological processing
  • 31-60: Intermediate - Slight difficulty, possible preventive intervention
  • 61-85: Moderate Risk - Significant deficit, monitoring recommended
  • >85: High Risk of Dyslexia - Urgent intervention recommended FLUENCY SCORE (0-100)
  • 0-30: Very Slow - WPM > 2 SDs below normal for age
  • 31-60: Moderate Slowness - WPM 1-2 SDs below
  • 61-85: Slightly Below Normal - WPM near average
  • >85: Adequate Fluency COMPREHENSION SCORE (0-100)
  • 0-30: Deficient - Comprehension significantly reduced
  • 31-60: Below Expected - Difficulty extracting meaning
  • 61-85: Adequate - Literal comprehension preserved
  • >85: Above Expected - Good access to meaning VISUAL PROCESSING SCORE (0-100)
  • 0-30: Deficient
  • 31-60: Intermediate
  • 61-100: Adequate AUDIOVISUAL DISCREPANCY INDEX (Important Marker)
  • Calculation: |Auditory Comprehension - Reading Comprehension|
  • >15 points: Strong indicator of specific dyslexia (reading processing vs. language)
  • <15 points: Possible general language deficit

OUTPUTS (Final Result)

1. Diagnostic Classification (STAGE 5)

DYSLEXIA DIAGNOSTIC CLASSIFICATION

No Dyslexia Risk · Overall Score 0–30
  • Action: Annual monitoring
Mild Risk · Overall Score 31–50
  • Action: Preventive intervention; retest in 3 months
Moderate Risk · Overall Score 51–70
  • Action: Specialist referral, reading intervention; retest in 1–2 months
High Risk / Probable Dyslexia · Overall Score 71–85
  • Action: Complete speech therapist evaluation; urgent educational referral
Confirmed / Severe Dyslexia · Overall Score > 85
  • Action: Neurologist referral; adapted school action plan

BUSINESS RULES SPECIFIC TO DYSLEXIA

Screening and Referral Rules

AUTOMATIC REFERRAL TO SPECIALIST IF:
  • Phonological Score > 70 and WPM < normal-1SD
  • Audiovisual discrepancy > 15 points persistent
  • 3+ alert indicators present
  • Guardian requests referral PROGRAM EXCLUSION IF:
  • Primary intellectual disability identified (dyslexia is reading-specific, not global)
  • Uncorrected hearing loss (confounds phonological processing)
  • Blindness or severe visual impairment
  • Trauma or acute mental health disorder INCLUSION WITH SPECIAL ACCOMMODATIONS IF:
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • May have dyslexia; assess separately
  • ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder)
  • May mask dyslexia; requires careful assessment of attention vs. decoding
  • Multilingual background
  • Assess in both languages; patterns must be consistent
  • Secondary dyslexia (post-trauma, stroke sequelae)
  • Assess with neuropsychologist

Severity Classification Rules

MILD DYSLEXIA (Score 51-60)
  • Action: Monitoring + Pedagogical Intervention
  • Frequency: 2-3x per week
  • Duration: 3-6 months
  • Responsible: Educator + Volunteer
  • Reassessment: 4 weeks MODERATE DYSLEXIA (Score 61-75)
  • Action: Intensive Intervention + Speech Therapist Referral
  • Frequency: 4-5x per week (20-30 min)
  • Duration: 6-12 months
  • Responsible: Educator + Reading Specialist + Speech Therapist
  • School accommodations: Extra time, dyslexia-friendly font, oral reading
  • Reassessment: 2-4 weeks SEVERE DYSLEXIA (Score >75)
  • Action: Neuropsychological Assessment + Professional Diagnosis
  • Frequency: 5x per week + specialized sessions 2x per week
  • Duration: 12+ months
  • Responsible: Multidisciplinary Team (Neuropsychologist, Speech Therapist, Reading Specialist)
  • Accommodations: Individualized plan, possible referral to special education
  • Reassessment: Weekly/biweekly

Rules of Dynamic Reclassification

PROMOTE TO HIGHER LEVEL (Less Risk) IF:
  • Score reduced > 10 points compared to last assessment
  • WPM increased > 20 words
  • 4+ weeks of consistent progress
  • Comprehension maintained or improved
  • Educator observation: "child more confident" DEMOTE TO LOWER LEVEL (More Risk) IF:
  • Score increased > 10 points (worsening)
  • WPM reduced (regression)
  • Adherence dropped <50%
  • New stressor event (school change, family bereavement)
  • Lack of responsiveness after 4 weeks of intervention SYSTEMIC PROBLEM INDICATOR IF:
  • >20% of cohort does not respond to standard intervention
  • Action: Review implementation quality, train educators
  • >10% dropout rate
  • Action: Analyze barriers (transportation, cost, stigma)
  • Bias patterns by gender/race
  • Action: Bias audit, stratify by demographics

SLA / SERVICE TIMELINE

STAGE 1: INITIAL IDENTIFICATION (Week 1)
  • Receive referral: <24 hours
  • Initial screening: <3 days
  • Evaluation scheduling: <1 week
  • Total: ~7 days STAGES 2-3: COGNITIVE EVALUATION (Weeks 1-2)
  • Session 1 (phonology + RAN): Day 1
  • Session 2 (WPM + Pseudo-words): Day 3
  • Session 3 (Comprehension + visual): Day 5
  • Data analysis: Day 6
  • Total: ~10 days for complete battery STAGES 4-5: DIAGNOSTIC AND PROFILE (Day 7)
  • Processing by AI: <2 minutes
  • Generation of scores and classification: <1 minute
  • Validation by algorithm: <1 minute
  • Total: ~5 minutes automatic STAGE 6: INTERVENTION PLAN (Day 7-8)
  • Elaboration by Hermes Agent: ~3-5 minutes
  • Review by reading specialist: ~20-30 minutes
  • Final approval: <1 hour
  • Total: ~1-2 hours for finalized plan STAGE 7: NOTIFICATIONS (Day 8-9)
  • Sent to parents: <1 hour after approval
  • Sent to educators: <1 hour
  • Notification to specialist (if >70): <2 hours
  • Total: Same day as approval STAGE 8: ACTIVE INTERVENTION (Weeks 3-24)
  • Intervention begins: Within 5 days of approval
  • Frequency: According to intensity (2-5x per week)
  • Continuous monitoring: Weekly/biweekly
  • Total: 6+ months of active program STAGE 9: EXIT EVALUATION (Weeks 24-28)
  • Complete retest: Sessions 1-3 (same duration)
  • Progression analysis: ~10 minutes
  • Impact report: ~20 minutes
  • Discussion with stakeholders: ~1 hour
  • Total: ~1-2 weeks for closure TOTAL PROGRAM: 6-7 months typical duration

SPECIFIC RISKS FOR DYSLEXIA

Risk Impact Mitigation
False Negative (miss child with dyslexia) Loss of critical intervention window Validation with 2 specialists; reassessment at 3 months
False Positive (diagnosing dyslexia when it does not exist) Stigma, psychological distress Specificity >90% of the algorithm; clinical review
Language Bias Dyslexia detection in non-Latin languages impaired Assessment in native language; language-specific norms
Undetected Comorbidity (ADHD, ASD) Incomplete diagnosis Combined ADHD/ASD screening questions
Loss of Child Engagement Child abandons program Gamification, positive reinforcement, clear progression
Inadequate School Accommodations Child continues struggling despite intervention Feedback loop with school; dynamic accommodation adjustment
